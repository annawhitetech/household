3. Long Dream

ddddddddddddd likened himself to THE SCARECROW.

I read about a patient with disordered dreaming. Twenty-six months to a night's sleep. Then twenty-six centuries, then longer still. By his penultimate rest, the patient's body has undergone the next four million years of human evolution: his straw skin, his pulsing orb eyes, his face swollen beyond the elasticity of the human skull. a sleep that stays. In the morning, NURSE opens a window. The innocent breeze turns the Long Dreamer’s crystallized body to dust.

THE SCARECROW, dddddddddddd claimed, knew “All that had and all that would.” then added that such knowledge was too overwhelming. It couldn’t be processed in the elasticity of human understanding. Thus, THE SCARECROW, who knew everything, could know nothing.

Later, as an insult, dddddddddddddddd likened me to TOTO. I found it more appropriate than insulting. TOTO was the only one who didn’t see Oz in color.

(Under microscopes, the doctors found traces of an unidentifiable stimulant in the dreamer’s particles.)
